Roth Conversion Planner

Click Calculate Plan to see Roth conversion recommendations.
Roth Conversion Strategy: Choose how aggressively to convert traditional balances to Roth each year. "Fill the bracket" strategies convert just enough to reach the ceiling of a target bracket, avoiding unnecessary tax. Conversions reduce future RMDs and create tax-free growth, but cost tax today.

NYS Note: NYS does not have special Roth conversion rules — conversions are taxable at state level (partially offset by the $20k/person exclusion on retirement income).

Monte Carlo Stress Test

How it works: Your withdrawal plan is held constant, but each simulation runs with a different sequence of randomized annual market returns drawn from a normal distribution around your configured growth rates. This reveals how much your outcome depends on luck vs. the plan itself — especially sequence-of-returns risk (bad markets early in retirement are far more damaging than bad markets later).
⚡ Circuit Breakers (dynamic spending guardrails)
Spending adjusts each year based on your current withdrawal rate vs. your initial rate. A cut triggers when the rate rises too high (portfolio underperforming); a boost triggers when it falls too low (portfolio outperforming). The floor and ceiling are hard limits on how far spending can move from your base plan.
Stress test runs automatically with Calculate Plan.
Change volatility or simulations above and click Re-run to update.
Portfolio Value — Range of Outcomes
How to Read This

■ Top band (P75–P90): The best ~25% of market scenarios — strong, consistent returns throughout retirement.

■ Middle band (P25–P75): The most likely range of outcomes. This is where most retirement paths land.

■ Bottom band (P10–P25): Poor market conditions — sustained low returns or a significant early-retirement drawdown.

Success Rate = the percentage of simulations where the portfolio was never fully depleted. A rate above 85–90% is generally considered a robust plan.

Sequence-of-returns risk is the biggest driver of failure. A 30% market drop in year 1 of retirement is far more damaging than the same drop in year 15, because you're selling assets at depressed prices to cover expenses before they can recover.

⚡ Circuit breakers dynamically adjust spending when your withdrawal rate drifts too far from the initial rate. This protects the portfolio in bad markets and lets you enjoy more in good ones.
